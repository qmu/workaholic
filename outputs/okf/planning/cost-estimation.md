# Cost Estimation Methods

_Estimate costs on explainable foundations, account for AI productivity variability, state scope boundaries clearly, and treat estimates as starting points for agreement rather than announcements of a figure._

We estimate costs presented to clients on explainable foundations and show their contents in a visible form. For effort, we do not simply apply traditional person-month calculations but build them in a way that accounts for productivity variability in a structure where generative AI is the default author. On top of that, we make explicit what is included and what is separate, and where uncertainty remains, and we treat estimates not as notifications of a dollar amount but as starting points for reaching agreement with the client.

## Goal (目標)

We aim for a state in which the basis of estimates remains in an explainable form, and both we and the client can trace why a cost is what it is. We aim for a state where what is included and what is not, and how much uncertainty is assumed where, are visible, and the interpretation of scope is not misaligned between us and the client.

## Responsibility (責務)

Our responsibility is to prevent a state in which estimates whose basis cannot be explained, or single figures that hide uncertainty, are converted as-is into promises to the client.

Estimates always carry premises not yet fully verified and remaining uncertainty. AI productivity varies depending on how much ground can be verified — it moves quickly in areas where types, tests, and scripts are in place, while human verification time returns in areas without scaffolding. Averaging this variance and putting out a low figure only on the "it'll be fast" premise, then converting a thinly-supported amount into a promise as-is, causes misinterpretation of scope to surface as "that was supposed to be included" once work is underway.

## Practices (実践)

### Build estimates with explainable basis

We build estimates in a form that retains the breakdown of work, the range assumed for each, and the premises placed. We value being able to trace where a figure comes from more than the figure itself, and we maintain a state where, when a premise changes, it is clear which part of the estimate to recalculate.

### Account for AI productivity variability

The speed AI can produce varies between areas where the verifiable ground is in place and areas where it is not. We place the presence or absence of scaffolding like types, tests, and scripts as premises of the estimate, and we budget for verification time in areas with thin scaffolding. The scaffolding-building itself is also treated as part of the effort.

### Clarify what is included and excluded

We make explicit what is included and what is not, what is treated as one package and what is separate. By not leaving scope boundaries vague, we maintain a state where the client can verify the contents of the estimate.

### Show uncertainty as ranges

We show uncertainty that has not yet been fully verified as a range rather than rounding it to a single figure. We share with the client where uncertainty remains and then consult on how to proceed with agreement.

### Document agreements

Agreements made with the client around estimates are left in a form that both parties can trace afterward. Estimates agreed in meetings are also noted in the meeting record.
