# Industry Research and Market Survey

_Study the client's industry, market, and actual business operations before starting a project, as the foundation from which design and implementation proceed._

Before beginning a project, we make it our starting point to research and understand the industry and market in which the client's business operates, as well as the actual state of the client's own work — from our side, on our own initiative. Industry customs, market structure, the movements of peers, the workflows and pain points that clients have not yet put into words, and the proprietary terminology that is taken for granted in the field — these are all things we want to bring back from this research.

## Goal (目標)

We aim for a state in which the client's business, and the structure of the industry and market surrounding it, are grasped as our own understanding, and in which the design and implementation that follow stand on that understanding. We aim for a state in which we can trace back to why a given request emerged — seeing the workflow and pain behind it, and the position of that business within its market.

## Responsibility (責務)

Our responsibility is to prevent a state in which we take clients' words and immediate requests at face value and proceed to requirements and implementation without verifying the actual business and market context.

A stated request is often a partial expression of the business and market context behind it. Taking only the words as given tends to cause a drift between what we are building and the problem we are trying to solve. In a firm where generative AI is our default author, there is a tendency to fall into a state where fragments are given and AI fills in requirements by inference, with those inferences flowing into design and implementation without being verified.

## Practices (実践)

### Research industry and market structure

We study the customs and business practices of the industry the client belongs to, relevant regulations, the movements of peers, and the size and direction of the market. We understand the client's requests in the context of where their business sits within the market, going as far as understanding what drives competitive advantage for that business.

### Capture unarticulated client structures

Requests that clients bring to us are expressions of a part of their actual business. We look and ask beyond the stated words to find what kind of workflow a request emerged from, who is involved, and where the effort and pain lies. We treat as subjects of research the premises the client has not yet put into words and the procedures shared implicitly in the field.

### Bring back proprietary terminology as research output

The names and expressions that are taken for granted in an industry and field reflect the structure of that business and market. In our research we gather these proprietary expressions and bring them back, ready to use as starting points for the ubiquitous language in subsequent design. See [Codifying Domain Terminology](/planning/terminology.md) for details.

### Use recordings and materials as research scaffolding

We retain what was exchanged in research and the materials provided by the client in a form that can be traced afterward. See the related policies on meeting recordings and materials requests.
