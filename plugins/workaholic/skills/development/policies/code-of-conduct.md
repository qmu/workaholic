# Code of Conduct

_The good-faith obligations that developers participating in a project fulfil: client-first decision-making, truthful and objective explanation, confidentiality, respect for diversity, and care for health._

Developers participating in development fulfil the following good-faith obligations.

## Goal (目標)

We aim for a state in which every developer participating in a project acts with client benefit as their first priority — within the constraints of law, good faith, and social norms — and where technical discretion, objective explanation, confidentiality, respectful collaboration, and attention to health are all maintained as a matter of course. We aim for a state in which the organization's decisions and explanations are traceable and the trust placed in us by clients is backed by the actual conduct of each developer.

## Responsibility (責務)

Our responsibility is to prevent a state in which personal interests — whether active (technical enthusiasm, career-building) or passive (avoiding difficult problems, deprioritizing low-visibility work) — are prioritized over client benefit in technical decisions. We prevent a state in which the information asymmetry between technical and non-technical people is exploited, explanations are distorted for personal convenience, or confidential information is handled carelessly. We also prevent a state in which individuals in poor health continue to work without seeking help, or where lack of respect in collaboration damages those involved.

## Practices (実践)

### Act with client benefit first

Except where law, good faith, or social norms prohibit it, developers think and act with client benefit first. Developers are given greater or lesser technical discretion depending on their role. In exercising that discretion, developers pay particular attention to two points.

**Do not prioritize internal enthusiasm over client benefit.** Decisions whose motivation partly includes "personal interest" — such as technical curiosity or career-building — call for care. We respect internal motivation itself and expect it to have a positive effect on development. However, technical decisions are to be made with the conviction that they are always the best option in terms of client benefit, or made after sufficiently explaining the trade-offs.

**Do not prioritize internal passivity over client benefit.** Developers tend toward passivity on problems whose resolution involves risk or difficulty, problems that are troublesome, and issues that do not easily connect to direct evaluation. There are certainly cases where there are other problems that should take business priority, or where careful handling is required. On the other hand, blindly pushing problems back can damage client benefit over the medium and long term. Developers maintain a stance of engaging actively with these problems as well, and even when pushing a problem back, act with the conviction that it is the best option in terms of client benefit.

In both cases, the developer as decision-maker being aware is not enough — the priority of client benefit is maintained as a development organization by mutually checking through review and other means.

### Ensure truthfulness and explain with objectivity

Developers may be asked for technical explanations by non-technical people. However, non-technical people cannot judge the truth or falsehood of those explanations. Unfortunately, taking advantage of this, developers may sometimes distort explanations for their own convenience. This must be avoided.

For example, when a problem occurs through one's own negligence, the impulses of treating it as unseen, of not chasing it deeply, of subjectively minimizing the damage — these can pass through one's head. Developers confront these mental movements squarely and ensure truthfulness, explaining with objectivity.

Of course, the more serious the problem, the more multiple engineers engage with it and a mutual monitoring effect against misconduct is expected, but even without that, engineers execute their duties fairly. All responsibility for trouble lies with management, and no personal penalty is imposed on developers. Developers can act with peace of mind, from a technician's pride and conscience.

### Comply with confidentiality obligations and work to protect confidential information

Developers are not only bound by confidentiality obligations themselves but are also required to maintain the security of confidential information in development projects. At a time when information leaks have long been a risk that can affect a company's survival, this is no overstatement to call a heavy responsibility.

In this guideline, rather than demanding that kind of heavy responsibility from individual developers, it is positioned as organizational responsibility, and we work toward risk reduction through systematization such as rules and procedures.

However, that "systematization" often involves trade-offs with convenience, and considering balance with development efficiency, a certain degree of compromise is allowed. And this compromise often rests on trust in individual developers.

All responsibility for trouble lies with management, and no personal penalty is imposed on developers. Developers need not feel anxiety about this point, but we require a thorough understanding of the importance of confidentiality obligations and protection of confidential information, care without complacency, and constant attention to preventing information leak incidents.

### Respect diversity and individuality, and collaborate with decorum

Developers collaborate with people of diverse backgrounds. In no case is discrimination based on race, nationality, sexual orientation, religion, political belief, physical characteristics, age, disability, or other attributes permitted.

Software development is also intellectual work and joint work. Sometimes carefully thought-out code is negatively reviewed. Schedules press, fatigue accumulates, and one cannot always be in good spirits.

While some degree of conflict is unavoidable, developers aim for communication with decorum and constructive discussion even at such times. Every person is perhaps a proud son or daughter, a proud husband or wife, a proud father or mother at home. No one should be hurt beyond business and technology.

### Immediately resolve situations that damage physical or mental health

Software development is sometimes harsh. Even with careful planning and appropriate schedule management, developers can be rushed by emergency responses or exhausted by extended handling.

If such a state continues, it may develop into a situation that damages physical and mental health. Since management bears responsibility for maintaining an appropriate working environment, measures will be taken to resolve such situations.

At the same time, we also require developers themselves to give sufficient daily attention to the physical and mental health of themselves and those around them. Specifically, we ask that you watch the health condition of yourself and those around you, and consult early if there are signs or symptoms of trouble. Since management bears responsibility for role changes, filling vacancies, and plan changes, developers can consult with their manager with peace of mind.
