# Upfront IT Investment Evaluation

_Evaluate IT investment from a return-on-investment perspective before proposals, exploring with clients the appropriate scale of investment rather than maximizing order size._

Software and services proposed to clients are assessed for their value through quantitative IT investment evaluation before work begins. Our aim is not to maximize order size; rather, we make it our approach to explore together with the client, and with the client's judgment, the scale of investment that is appropriate from a return-on-investment perspective. We view system development as an investment for the client — one that has a return as its goal while carrying the possibility of losing the amount invested.

## Goal (目標)

We aim for a state in which the client can share with us the materials to judge whether their IT investment aligns with business value. We aim for a state in which it is visible how much is invested in what, what kind of effect can be expected to what degree of certainty, and where even the room to phase the scale of investment is visible together.

## Responsibility (責務)

Our responsibility is to prevent a state in which we recommend investment while leaving the return on investment vague, in order to increase order size.

There are situations where the interests of us who take on the development and the client who invests are not aligned. While increasing the amount built increases our order size, there is a point where investment stops being aligned with business value for the client. To protect both the investor and the worker, we want to avoid proposals that hide the expected effect and its certainty.

## Practices (実践)

### Evaluate from ROI, not order size

We place the starting point of proposals not in our order size but in the client's business value. We show how much investment in what yields what kind of effect to what certainty, and we draw together with the client the outline of a scale that makes investment worthwhile. We consult in the direction of keeping things within the appropriate scope, not in the direction of increasing the amount built.

### Phase investment to reduce uncertainty

In areas where the effect of investment is not fully readable, we propose a way of proceeding that verifies in small increments before investing big all at once.

### Ground in business understanding

The assessment of return on investment stands on understanding the client's business and the industry and market surrounding it. Based on the business reality grasped through [Industry Research and Market Survey](market-research.md), we estimate where effects will land.
